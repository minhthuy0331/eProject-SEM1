The Current Situation

In the world today, there are more than 1556 species that are extinct and in danger of disappearing from the list of wild animals. The main cause of this extinction is illegal hunting by humans. The explosion of population as well as the development of tools of labor has led people to destroy nature to expand their living. In Vietnam is one of the places where there is a diversity of rare animals. However, this diversity is gradually being destroyed. Rare and precious animals such as birds, cranes, deer, elephants... are illegally poached, causing many species to be in danger of extinction. Click on each project to learn more.

The Amur (the Manchurian Leopard)

Is a wild carnivore native to the mountainous areas of the Taiga forest as well as other temperate forests in Korea , East and South Korea. North China and the Far East of Russia. It is one of the rarest cat species in the world with an estimated 30-35 individuals remaining in the wild.

Dolphin Vaquita

Is a species of porpoise endemic to the northern end of the Gulf of California in Baja California, Mexico. Averaging 150 cm (4.9 ft) (females) or 140 cm (4.6 ft) (males) in length, it is the smallest of all living cetaceans. The species is currently on the brink of extinction, and currently listed as Critically Endangered by the IUCN Red List; the steep decline in abundance is primarily due to bycatch in gillnets from the illegal totoaba fishery.

The Orangutan

Is a genus of the human family (large apes), of the extant order of Primates in Asia . They are large animals, living more in trees than on the ground. A dark yellow mixed with gray bushy primate, adapted only to tropical life on the islands of Borneo and Sumatra in Indonesia. They are among the closest to humans both physically and intellectually.

Panthera tigris sondaica

Sunda tigers are distinguished by the thick black stripes on their orange fur. There are only 400 individuals left, all living on the island of Sumatra. Conservationists worry that deforestation and poaching (accounting for 80% of tiger deaths) could lead to their extinction, similar to what happened to their Javan and Balinese colleagues .

Learn more

Sumatran elephant

Is a subspecies of the Asian elephant found only on the Indonesian island of Sumatra . They are the second smallest subspecies, only 1.7 to 2.6 meters tall at the shoulder. They are sometimes called pocket elephants because of their small size. Female elephants are smaller than males and have no tusks.

Learn more

Tortoise(Eretmochelys imbricata )

is a species of sea turtle in the family Cheloniidae. It is the only species in the genus Eretmochelys . This species is distributed worldwide, with two subspecies Atlantic and Pacific . Eretmochelys imbricata imbricata is an Atlantic subspecies, while Eretmochelys imbricata bissa is found in the Indo-Pacific region.
